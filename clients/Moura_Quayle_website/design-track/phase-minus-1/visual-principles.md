# Visual Principles - Moura Quayle Living Archive

## Core Visual Principles

### 1. Scholarly Vibrance
**Principle**: Merge academic credibility with artistic energy
- Typography that commands respect while feeling approachable
- Color that energizes without overwhelming content
- Layouts that organize complexity while inviting exploration

### 2. Living Dynamism
**Principle**: The archive breathes and evolves
- Subtle animations that suggest ongoing thought
- Interactive elements that reward curiosity
- Visual cues that distinguish static archive from active musings

### 3. Integrated Wholeness
**Principle**: Visual unity across diverse content types
- Consistent treatment of academic papers, artwork, and musings
- Seamless transitions between Moura's and David's work
- Cohesive experience from dense text to visual galleries

### 4. Personal Authenticity
**Principle**: Moura's unique voice shines through
- Cool, bright colors drawn from her artistic palette
- Watercolor textures that connect to her art practice
- Typography that reflects both precision and creativity

### 5. Accessible Excellence
**Principle**: Beautiful doesn't compromise usability
- High contrast for readability
- Clear navigation patterns
- Responsive design that maintains elegance
- Performance that respects users' time

## Visual Hierarchy

### Primary Level: Discovery
- Homepage hero introducing the Living Archive concept
- Clear pathways to major sections
- Immediate sense of breadth and depth

### Secondary Level: Exploration
- Section landings that orient users
- Filtering and search prominence
- Visual differentiation between content types

### Tertiary Level: Engagement
- Article/artwork presentation
- Related content connections
- Interaction opportunities

## Emotional Targets

Users should feel:
- **Inspired**: By the integration of art and academia
- **Curious**: To explore connections between works
- **Respected**: Through thoughtful information architecture
- **Energized**: By the vibrant yet sophisticated aesthetic
- **Connected**: To the person behind the work

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- ✓ Use color purposefully to guide and delight
- ✓ Respect the gravity of academic work
- ✓ Celebrate the joy in artistic expression
- ✓ Make complex information digestible
- ✓ Enable serendipitous discovery

### Don'ts
- ✗ Let vibrancy become visual noise
- ✗ Sacrifice readability for aesthetics
- ✗ Create artificial separation between work types
- ✗ Overwhelm with too many choices
- ✗ Make assumptions about user pathways

## Success Metrics
- Users spend >5 minutes exploring (engagement)
- <2 seconds to understand site purpose (clarity)
- 90%+ accessibility score (inclusivity)
- Positive emotional response in testing (inspiration)
- Easy citation/sharing of materials (utility)
